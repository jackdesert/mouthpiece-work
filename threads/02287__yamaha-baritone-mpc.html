FROM: wyznerd (wyznerd)
SUBJECT: Re: yamaha baritone mpc
I wouldn't waste the time trying to put a Yamaha 5C back to original 
specs.  They are horrible mouthpieces in original spec.  I've redone 
several of them and made very good sounding pieces but they are very 
brittle plastic and chip if you even look at them hard.  I've had the 
tips chip just from the pressure of refacing them on fine sandpaper 
and glass.  However, I've got two that I've reworked and are really 
great playing pieces now -- I worry about selling them though, since 
they chip so easily. 

I don't remember the exact tip opening but it was very narrow - 
maybe .075" (about 1.92mm)  I opened one up to .105" and the other 
to .118 (the .105 really needs to be opened up more I think).

FROM: ed_svoboda (esvoboda@...)
SUBJECT: Re: yamaha baritone mpc
Some of it depends on chamber size in relation to tip opening in my opinion.  The Morgan 1C is even smaller than .077 at .068.  My vintage pieces can measure even more closed than the Morgan!  The old Buescher pieces were .063 for their medium facing piece (facing length was 25.4mm).  

The spec for the 5C is .082 or 2.10mm.  For classical on this piece it might be better with a tip opening a bit larger say up to .095.  It really depends on what the player wants to do with it and what kind of chops they have.
