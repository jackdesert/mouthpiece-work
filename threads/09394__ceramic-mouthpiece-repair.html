One of my customers dropped his Drake ceramic mouthpiece, and it took  
an unlucky bounce and damaged the tip/siderail junction....I've got to  
fill it and reface it, and am curious if any of you have found a  
filler material that works better than others. I'm thinking this sort  
of fragility is a real downside to this material.

BTW, I've played a couple of these, and they were nicely finished and  
played very well.

FROM: teoenwy (Tony F.)
SUBJECT: Re: Ceramic mouthpiece repair
I've never epaired a mouthpiece with it, but I've used an epoxy adhesive with a filler to repair statuary. I used Araldite and a tile grout of the appropriate colour and it produces an extremely hard repair that works easily and finishes well. You'd have to consider toxicity, it wasn't an issue for me but it might be for you. Alternatively, explore the area of dental fillers.
Tony F.
