The beauty in the Benade/Gebler calculations is that the use of a simple cylindrical tube allows an accurate measurement of the "equivalent" volume of the mouthpiece.

In other words the volume, when added to the cylinder, would produce a resonant frequency the same as the one played using the mouthpiece added to the cylinder.

Trying to use a cone would not achieve a better result.  It would just make it harder to find or make the components used in the test.  

Finding the "equivalent volume" of the mouthpiece is actually a process by which one can estimate the volume added by the space taken by the vibrating reed and the interaction with the player's oral cavity. 

The comparison of the equivalent volume of the mouthpiece with the estimated volume of the missing cone the mouthpiece is said to replicate under ideal conditions is a totally separate calculation than the one done in my simple study.  For this reason, the shape of the tube used is not an issue.
