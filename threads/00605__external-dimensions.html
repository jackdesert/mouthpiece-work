FROM: danny_tb (Danny Barrett <danny_tb@...>)
SUBJECT: Re: External dimensions
Nick,
It's interesting that you should say that. When Stan Getz (yes, I'm 
on about him again...) went to the Meyer mcp near the end of his 
life, he did so because he wanted a thinner beak, so that it would 
fit his mouth better (most of his life he struggled with the 
uncomfortable bulkiness (as far as he was concerned) of the HR Otto 
Link - and he himself said that he would prefer a metal mouthpiece, 
but none of them would give him the results that he wanted - material 
has a difference for some people it would seem).

However, when he went to the Otto Link guys about it, they wouldn't 
thin down the beak of a mouthpiece for him because (as far as their 
opinions were concerned) it would change the tone of the piece (hence 
he got the specially customised Meyer instead).

Fron this, it seems that outside dimensions do have something to do 
with the tone of the mouthpiece if they change its resonating 
characteristics.

DB
