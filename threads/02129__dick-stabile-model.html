SUBJECT: Re: dick stabile model?
Ralph Morgan listed some "specs" of vintage mouthpieces in his 
March/April 1993 Sax Journal column.  I think Paul Coats said this 
info came from an earlier edition of the Erik Brand book.  

I have checked a few original-looking Woodwind and Link mouthpieces 
against the numbers posted and have not found good agreement.  
Mouthpieces just vary a lot.  Here is what was listed for a Dick 
Stabile Alto:

42157 (Erik Brand designation: Length mm*2 & Tip mm)
42
29
20
13
6
157 (= .062" tip)

Erik Brand feeler set:
.0015, .010, .024, .034, .050"

FROM: tenorman1952 (Paul Coats)
SUBJECT: Re: dick stabile model?
This compare quite well with the specs in the old Eric Brand manual:

Cool Baffle's (E. Brand)

42,4          (42 E Brand)
30             (29 E Brand)
20             (20 E Brand)
13             (13 E Brand)
6               (6  E Brand)
tip opening: 1.56 mm (.061)    (157 E Brand)

This is well within what would be expected of normal production 
tolerances, and confirms the validity of the old E. Brand numbers.
