I worked at a jewelery/pawn shop once (so I could get first dibs on the instruments that came out of pawn) -  and they always sent their buffing wheels (and all the buffing compound residue and lint around the buffer) out have the gold/silver reclaimed. Sorry that I don't know the details of where they sent it. I also remember back in the early days of computers, most all chips had gold contacts - when defective chips were replaced, the old chips were sent to have the gold reclaimed. At least the computer repairman I knew sent them out - he serviced DEC equipment and always had buckets full of bad chips with gold plated contacts that he was sending somewhere to be reclaimed.
