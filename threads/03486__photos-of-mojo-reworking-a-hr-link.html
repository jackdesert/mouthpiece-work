> >Pro glass gauges are not available here in Scotland and transparent
labels are expensive, so I made my gauge from picture glass (free
cuttings  from a framer) with a 1mm scale from a clear Helix acrylic
ruler. The scale with its sloping edge is cut away from the ruler.
From this, two scales are cut to fit the glass size, 35x70mm in my
case, with their thin edges and markings, lined up, face to face down
the centre of the glass.
These are glued along the outer edges with medium or thick
cyano-acrylate (ZAP), taking care not to allow the glue to migrate on
to the scale area. A little care has to be taken in its use and the 1mm 
segments have to be transposed into real measurements, but it has
proved perfectly adequate for an amateur refacer.             Eddie
