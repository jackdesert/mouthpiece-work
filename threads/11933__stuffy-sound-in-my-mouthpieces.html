FROM: kwbradbury (Keith Bradbury)
SUBJECT: Re: stuffy sound in my mouthpieces
If the response is not clear, this can come from flat spots on the facing curve.  This is true for sax mouthpieces but mant clarinet mouthpieces sound fine with flat spots.  It depends on where they are located.   Crooked spots may also be a source of response problems.  Then there is the degree of facing curvature which and make a mouthpiece stuffy to free blowing and everything in between.
If you supply some facing measurements along with the feeler sizes you are using, I (and others here) can give you some recommendations. Mojo Mouthpiece Work LLC

SUBJECT: Re: stuffy sound in my mouthpieces
Stuffy...this can also come from 

--the tip rail being too wide

--no baffle, that is, the baffle does not leave the tip rail in a small curve, but rather 
a straight line, so there is a sharp angle at the tip rail.
