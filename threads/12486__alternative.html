FROM: kwbradbury (Keith Bradbury)
SUBJECT: Re: Alternative tip gauges
There is a photo album on the Yahoo group site that shows several tip measuring gauges.  One of them is the machinist's depth gauge I use that has a 6" base.  I can see the tip rail fine when using it.   But I still probe around a little to convince myself what the reading should be.  Tip measuring takes a bit of "art" to get a good reading.  More "art" than a gauge reading should need.   

I find the spring loaded plunger can give you a false large reading when measuring softer plastic mouthpieces.  Delrin Quantums are tricky to measure.  You need to hold back the plunger and kiss it up to the tip rail.   With a machinist's gauge, there is no spring loaded plunger.

https://groups.yahoo.com/neo/groups/MouthpieceWork/photos/albums/1539473094
