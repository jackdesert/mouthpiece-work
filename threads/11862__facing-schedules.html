FROM: kwbradbury (Keith Bradbury)
SUBJECT: Re: Facing-schedules
Each 5* measures different.  Either from wear/alteration over time or manufacturing variation.   You can measure a few if you can get your hands on any.  This is why this group tends to calculate curves based on tip opening and facing length targets for a particular set of feelers.

I have a scan of a Sax Journal article Morgan wrote where he listed a bunch of (now vintage) facing schedules for the Brand feelers.  I was never able to get measurements of pristine vintage mouthpieces to agree with these charts.  But I can send it to you if you wish.

At one time I was going to upload a lot of Morgan's SJ articles to the Yahoo group site.  But SJ did not grant permission to do this.  They are probably still selling back issues so I doubt they will give permission until there is no one left to ask.

FROM: mike_wilkens2000 ()
SUBJECT: Re: Facing-schedules
Your numbers represent a good radial curve as long as you aim for a tip measurement of .085 measured at the OUTSIDE edge of the tip rail. 

 I mention it because a lot of guys measure tips at the inside of the tip rail. When you do this, your targets have to be adjusted for your target tip rail thickness if you want a radial curve.
 

 According to the Bradbury/Donaldson Spreadsheet I use, If you aim for a .030" tip rail and .085" at the inside of the tip rail, your targets should be more like:
 .0015  24.3
 .010    18.6
 .024    13.5
 .034    10.7
 .050     7.1
