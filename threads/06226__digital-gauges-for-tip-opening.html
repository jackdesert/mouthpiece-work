I just bought two digital gauges from member David Kulcinski.  These 
are used, but good working gauges.  These are the "Logic Basic 
Series" made by CDI Chicago.  

The gauges can be set for mm's or inches.

They fit perfectly in the fixtures I got from Steve Fowler and my 
original Winslow tip gauge.

The neat thing... with the needle type gauges, I had to count 
backwards to determine the tip opening as the probe dropped below the 
zero (which was the plane of the mouthpiece table).  

With these digital gauges I can place the gauge/fixture on my glass 
work surface, zero the gauge.  Then place on the mouthpiece and read 
the tip opening as, for example, -.098".  No counting back, no 
subtraction, just a direct reading.

I'll let David post purchase details.  

I liked the first one so much I bought a second one.
