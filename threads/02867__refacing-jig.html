FROM: rosss12000 (Ross Smith)
SUBJECT: Re: Refacing Jig
Hi Ross
If you're referring to my post of Jan 28, showing a home made jig, It's not worth the effort.
It's very hard to hold the piece still enough.
The paper loads quickly, and then cuts unevenly.
You have very little control over lay length..

It does swing nicely through an arc, but would be much more useful as a clock pendulum.

FROM: kwbradbury (Keith Bradbury)
SUBJECT: Re: Refacing Jig
Keep in mind that precision on the order of .0001" is needed to get the
side rails even.  A tilting feeler gage illustrates the uneveness.

It is difficult to make a jig with moving parts that can hold a mouthpiece
so well that is does not shift around by more than .0001".
