Actually most big band sax players (alto at least) will double on clarinet
and flute as a matter of necessity.  And in my senior year of high school, I
was bored and decided to play contrabass clarinet in the symphonic band.
The darn thing nearly shook my face off in the lower register.. But I never
remember being able to pick up a girl because of being a contrabass clarinet
player.  So needless to say my contrabass days were quite limited!  ;-)
FROM: zoot51 (Bill Hausmann)
SUBJECT: Re: Learning About Mouthpieces
Actually, MANY saxophonists play clarinet.  A lot, like me, started on clarinet and later picked up sax because of the similarities.  Since sax fingering is actually SIMPLER than clarinet, it is much easier to move from clarinet to sax than from sax to clarinet.

Bill Hausmann
