FROM: kwbradbury (Keith Bradbury)
SUBJECT: Re: Classic hard rubber mouthpiece
I try to get my clients to describe sound changes they want as brighter/darker and focused/spread (fat).   Warmth can come from a more spread sound.  I might make the the throat larger.   

But this may not be needed if there are facing issues that need to be corrected.  I tell my clients that facing changes primarily address response.  But if the mouthpiece responds better for them, they may be able to sculpt the sound more to their liking without chamber modifications.  

Occasionally a client asked for a more focused sound which I think comes from a smaller chamber.  But they don't like it and the chamber needs to be enlarged in a follow-up adjustment.  I use temporary putty as a test when I can to see if they like going in that direction and whether they want more/less.

FROM: frymorgan ()
SUBJECT: Re: Classic hard rubber mouthpiece
Sure, the request is kind of vague, but many players think of the sound in terms like this so if you're charging money then figuring this stuff out is part of the job IMO. Talk to your guy some more. If they're local it's easiest, but a phone call is always better than email. You have to be able to translate these concepts into something actionable, whether it's on Mojo's bright/dark vs. focused/spread space, or however it is you think about it. 

 All that aside, the Concept is basically the old Airflow, and Selmer has a preference for short facings. Your guy may need a larger chambered piece or a longer and/or flatter facing. The precise combination of acoustic, aerodynamic, and mechanical changes required I couldn't possibly guess without playing the piece.
