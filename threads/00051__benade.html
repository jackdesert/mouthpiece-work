FROM: jbtsax (John)
SUBJECT: Re: Benade
For those who are not familiar with Jim Gebler.  He worked alongside
Arthur Benade for 2 years in the 1970s at Case Western University and
remained a close friend of both Arthur and his wife Virginia until
Arthur's death in 1987.  Arthur Benade includes Jim Gebler in the
credits to his book "The Fundamentals of Musical Acoustics the most
widely used text on the subject, and a cornerstone of the writings in
musical acoustics. Jim's work on several woodwind projects is noted in
several places throughout the text.

Jim wrote a three part series in the Woodwind Quarterly entitled
"Diagnosing Woodwind Bores".  He has also written articles on instrument
repair for the Music Educator's Journal.

He and Benade collaborated in a study entitled "Reed cavity and neck
proportions in conical woodwinds" cited in the Journal of the Acoustic
Society of America in 1973.

Jim and his wife currently own and operate the Fine Band Instrument
Repair Shop in Guilford Connecticut and is highly regarded by top
woodwind players all over the country for his skill in diagnosing and
correcting acoustic deficiencies in high end woodwinds as well as his
artistry in repair.

Jim is the "real deal" who has not only the scientific background in
acoustics, but the real world experience of knowing how to apply that
knowledge to the instruments that come across his bench.

He would be a great addition to this forum, if his schedule permits, and
again I hope that he is shown the respect and courtesy a man of his
experience and credentials is due.
