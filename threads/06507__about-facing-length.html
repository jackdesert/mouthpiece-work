FROM: kwbradbury (Keith Bradbury)
SUBJECT: Re: About facing length

In practice, the facing length is the length to a .0015" feeler gage.  The ".0000" tangent point can be determined by fitting a curve to the facing curve data set.  I suppose there are ways to measure it, but it would be more trouble than it is worth.  
 
BTW, I have suspected for some time that Selmer and Vandoren might list their facing length targets as to the ".0000" point.  Theye dod not list their facing lengths very often... and there is a lot of variation among the Selmers.  But I have some charts I found on the web that list facing lengths that are all a little longer than I can measure with a .0015" feeler.
