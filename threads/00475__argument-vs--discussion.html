FROM: kwbradbury (MojoBari)
SUBJECT: Argument vs. Discussion
I'm not exactly sure what is the difference, but I have read that an argument tries to prove who is right whereas a discussion tries to prove what is right.  Arguments tend to be more emotional and less civil.  They also often have "baggage" carried over from previous arguments. 

Just remember this is a discussion forum.  It is OK to eventually arrive at a conclusion that is different compared where you started at the beginning of a discussion.  In fact, it should happen regularly IMO.
FROM: jbtsax (John)
SUBJECT: Re: Argument vs. Discussion
The word "argument" as used in logic or debate refers to a discussion intended to persuade .  It can also be defined as a series of cohesive statements leading from a premise to a conclusion.

Another more negative definition of argument is that it is a quarrel or dispute.  I meant "argument" in the logical sense when I wrote that to my friend Toby since we have had many discussions on acoustics "intended to persuade" over the years on SOTW.
