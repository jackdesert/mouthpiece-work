SUBJECT: Mouthpiece Nomenclature
Is there anywhere that shows a side view cutaway drawing of a typical
mouthpiece with the different areas identified relative to the
nomenclature sheet in the files area?  For example; a cutaway with
arrows indicating the bore start/end, crown/ball, the chamber
start/end, types of baffle and where the heights are measured from, etc.

Also, in the mouthpiece data sheet, there is a box for chamber end
shape.  What are the typical options?  I have mpcs that have contours
where the chamber meets the bore that are circular, straight sides
with curved top & bottom, straight,but angled (where the top is flat,
but not as wide as the bottom), flat on top & bottom and concave on
the sides.

Another question (maybe this should be a separate post):  Does it
matter whether the side rails are angled, or parallel?

Thank you,

David

This is for you to insert your own words.  It is difficult to capture 
all the 3D shapes with words or measurements.  But a few words are 
better than nothing.  Theo Wanne's site has a few chamber pictures 
and descriptions.

Do you mean the inner sidewalls.  I generally note if they are 
straight or curved.  Sometime I describe an "A" chamber if the walls 
are not parallel as found in Brilharts.
