What appears missing from all the accounts presented on the web is 
first-hand word from Dave Guardala.  His past partner, Jeff Powell, 
posts snippets of his version of the story from time-to-time on 
alt.music.saxophone.  Dave's ex-girlfriend appears to have posted 
there too.  Search for posts by "ansermetniac".  He also posted on 
SOTW a few months ago but they have been deleted from SOTW.  (I may 
have saved the posts.)

Jeff claims the Dave is still his partner but I do not see a way to 
confirm this.  Jeff has posted comments that indicate the recent 
products being offered under the Guardala name have been a surprise to 
him.  It looks like Dave is making deals without involving Jeff.  It 
is possible that some vendors are using the Guardala name without 
permission of all parties who have control of the name.  Legal action 
may or may not sort this out in the future.  

Steve's report make it sound like Dennis Bamber has some kind of 
contract to use the Guardala name.  There is a mouthpiece maker/seller 
in Germany who thinks he has rights to use it too.  Now saxes with the 
Guardala name are also appearing.  Are there contracts and are they 
sound?  We just do not know.  We do know there is some disagreement.

According to a post by Jeff Powell, the Guardala company was involved with
the initial production runs of the original Laser Trimmed Guardalas.  They
did some quality control.  Later runs sold by WW&BW did not have any
involvement of the Guardala company.  Also, the box they are packaged in
says "hand-finished" even though there is no hand finishing on the Laser
Trimmed Guardalas.

The new Guardalas appearing now (with the Rovner ligatures) at WW&BW appear
to have no involment of the Guardala company.  The company, per se, may
only exist on paper.  There is no "staff" at Guardala.  The new German-made
Guadalas look similar to the Laser Trimmed one in the US.  But I have only
seen pictures.  The new MB2 is not like the original hand-made MB2.  The
new one has a bullet chamber like a King.  They are just using the Guradala
and MB2 name for marketing.  They may be fine mouthpieces, but not the same
as the originals.
