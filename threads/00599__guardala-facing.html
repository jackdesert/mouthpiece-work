SUBJECT: Re: Guardala Facing
A few comments:

1. The Guardala facing supplied way back by Ross is not typical of 
Guardalas.  The piece may be a fluke, damaged, and/or the measurements 
have some errors in them.

2. Flat spots often make a sax mouthpiece sound edgy, even at low 
volumes.  It can be hard to get a pure tone out of it.  Flat sections 
near the tip make palm keys and altissimo more difficult to play.

3. Bumps add resisitance to the facing curve.  They make it more 
difficult to get the reed to speak at low volumes.  The reed has a 
tough time bending over the bumps and sealing well.
