FROM: teoenwy (Tony F)
SUBJECT: Re: File suggestions? [1 Attachment]
I've looked for a reference for it, but can't find it online. I bought a 
roll many years ago, so haven't needed to buy it since, so perhaps it's 
a product that no longer exists. To describe it, imagine a length of 
stiff, but still flexible material consisting of several layers of a 
dense woven hessian material sandwiched with layers of latex. The whole 
length is studded with densley applied metal staples punched through 
from one side only in close-set rows. They are held in place by the 
latex and one side of the material presents something like a dense metal 
brush.
Tony F.

filing to drive the pieces out from between the teeth.

Hold the file flat on the bench, push the end of the piece of material hard
against the file, parallel to the teeth, driving the filings from between
the teeth.

Like materials work best: Steel for steel, brass for brass etc.

Hope that helps.
