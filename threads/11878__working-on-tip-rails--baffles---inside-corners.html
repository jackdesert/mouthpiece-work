FROM: lancelotburt (MartinMods)
SUBJECT: Re: Working on tip rails, baffles & inside corners.
There is no one way to do anything.  

What works for me is to get a good facing and trim the side rails, then bring the baffle to where it is close to where it should be, and then to do the tip rail.  Play testing begins then and usually there are baffle/tip rail adjustments to make.  If they go well a refacing isn't necessary.  Sometimes it is though.  I never try to play a half finished facing and I don't make facing adjustments to compensate for baffle issues.  Once I have my curve, I leave it alone. All my tone/response adjustments are done on the baffle.  That usually takes longer, but I think I get better results keeping the geometry that determines mechanical forces (how the reed rolls up the facing curve as a result of embouchure and blowing air pressure) separate from the geometry that determines acoustical forces (how internal mouthpiece air pressure changes and Bernoulli force affect the reed).  That's just me, though.

I like the tip rail width to be even, corner to corner, with as little radius in the corners as possible.
