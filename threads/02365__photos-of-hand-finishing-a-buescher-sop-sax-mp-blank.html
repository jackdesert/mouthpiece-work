FROM: kwbradbury (Keith Bradbury)
SUBJECT: Re: eMachine tenor blanks
You guys surprised me!  We are in the 33-38 range now and the price 
will be below $20 each + s/h.

I'm thinking of leaving the beak flat since it will be easier for any 
of us who may want to glue on a tooth deck for comfort.

Also, be aware that there is some risk involved here.  The tolerances 
are in the .003"-.005" range at these prices.  I'll explore how much 
the price goes up for tighter tolerances, but the first priority is 
to keep the cost down.

Also, the surface finish may not be great.  This means more hand 
finishing.  I'll read the fine print and see what we are in for.

If I can tighten the specs and keep us around $20 each I'll do it.

I just added photos of the "Mojo23" mouthpiece blank to the Photos - 
CAD section.  I'll do a few more checks on the reed fit but I think 
this one is "it".  

The window length is 1/4" shorter and is more typical of tenor MPs 
now.  I added a small second bullet-like relief in the throat.  Looks 
kinda cool but the main purpose is to mark where I think the baffle 
should ramp down to from the tip.  I plan to hand file mine down to 
that level (its another .100" down).

FYI the inside is tapered by 1 deg on the sides.  The outside is 2 
degs.  The tip opening should be about .095", but this needs to be 
finished by hand to create a tip rail to match a reed curve.

I do not have a plating source established.  We'll have to share 
experiances here down the road.

I'm sure I have 20-30 hours of learning curve and design time into 
this thing.  Most of it was fun.

There are pictures on the Yahoo MouthpieceWork site under the CAD 
folder.  Look at the Mojo23 design.  I actually submitted a Mojo25 
design but the differences were subtle.

The beak is higher than the original design pictures.  There will be 
no bite plate supplied.

The blanks may play, but I doubt it.  Here are the areas I think will 
need rework:

1. The tip rail.  Needs to be filed to match the curve of a reed.  
Needs to be defined from the inside.

2. The baffle.  It is too high.  It should be filed down at 
least .100" where it goes into the throat.  But you can do this in 
stages and experiment.  The higher beak allows you to make a low 
baffle MP if you wish.

3. The Beak.  I think the top is too flat for comfort.  The corners 
may dig into your upper lip.  If so, just file a more comfortable 
shape using other MPs as a guide.  If you have the means, you can 
even machine in a bite plate recess or dovetail and make a bite plate 
to fit.  But a simple mouthpiece patch is all you really need.

4. The facing.  There will be a 5" radius facing curve on it open to 
about .095" or so at the tip.  I doubt this facing curve will be very 
good.  It will need to be hand finished.  This will effect the tip 
rail and baffle too.

5. The side rails should be wider than the reed near the tip.  They 
should be narrowed down some from the outside to match the width of 
standard reeds.

Lastly, the shop tolerances are in the .003"-.005" range.  If they 
use a lot of this range, or exceed it, the blank may look crooked or 
not symmetrical.  This is the big unknown until they arrive in early 
November.
